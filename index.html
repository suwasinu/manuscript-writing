The AI-assisted manuscript workflow

AI is a co-pilot at every stage — but you stay the author and the verifier.

1
Plan & outline
Brainstorm the story, build an IMRaD skeleton, define the key message. AI = sounding board.
2
Draft section-by-section
Feed AI your data/notes for each section. Never ask it to invent results or references.
3
Revise & tighten
Clarity, flow, conciseness, academic tone, journal word limits. AI = best at this.
4
Self-review
Ask AI to role-play a harsh Reviewer 2. Find gaps before the journal does.
5
VERIFY everything
Check every fact, number, and citation against the original source. AI hallucinates references.
6
Polish & submit
Cover letter, plain-language summary, response to reviewers. Disclose AI use per journal policy.
⚠️ The golden rule: AI accelerates the writing, not the thinking. You are accountable for every word, number, and citation in the final manuscript.

Do & Don't

✅ DO use AI to…

  • Improve clarity, flow & grammar
  • Tighten wordy text to journal limits
  • Translate / polish non-native English
  • Outline & structure your argument
  • Brainstorm titles and key messages
  • Simulate a peer reviewer's critique
  • Draft cover letters & reviewer responses
  • Explain a stats concept or reviewer comment

⛔ DON'T let AI…

  • Invent data, results, or p-values
  • Generate citations (it hallucinates them!)
  • Be listed as an author
  • Replace your reading of the literature
  • Write conclusions your data don't support
  • Process confidential/patient data in public tools
  • Make clinical or causal claims unchecked
  • Go undisclosed if the journal requires it
🔍 #1 risk = fake references. AI confidently produces real-looking citations that don't exist. Verify every single one in PubMed / the actual journal before submitting.
